## 1. Gate delta

UK entry gates = the same 6 gates, UK-localised, **plus** (EXPANSION-MODEL §3, verbatim
intent): the AU playbook has *repeated* (not just launched), **and** UK anchor-account
motion validated — **≥2 Seasonal Worker scheme operators or estate/enterprise accounts
signed as design partners *before* any consumer-side spend.** "Requires hire 2."

## 2. Entry-shape delta: B-led, not A-led

AU enters bottom-up (owner-operator dairy, Segment A first). UK inverts: **Segment B
leads** (PRICING-MODEL §8 — % + Season Pass primary; A-flat £90/£300 secondary
[MODELLED]). Why: UK enterprise density is deeper (65/35 mix, MARKET-SIZING §2) and the
seasonal-labour market is institutionally intermediated — only 7 Home Office–approved
**Seasonal Worker scheme operators** may sponsor seasonal workers; individual farms
cannot [V — gov.uk sponsor guidance 04/26; operators: Agri-HR, Concordia, Fruitful Jobs,
HOPS Labour Solutions, Pro-Force (horticulture); AG Recruitment + Fruitful Jobs
(poultry)]. The anchor design partners are drawn from: these operators, large estates,
and corporate/multi-site farming companies. Consumer-side (seeker marketing, paid
acquisition, broad employer outreach) spends **nothing** until ≥2 anchors are signed.

What we sell the anchors [OPINION]: matching + workforce infrastructure for the domestic
and year-round layer their schemes don't cover, plus Index-UK workforce data — NOT a
competing sponsorship channel. TopFarms does not sponsor visas.

## 3. Localisation deltas

- **Visa module — full rework again (AU logic does not port):**
  - **Seasonal Worker visa [V — 2026 rules]:** 42,900 places for 2026 (41,000
    horticulture + 1,900 poultry); max 6 months' work in any rolling 10-month period
    (tightened from 12 months, for CoS assigned on/after 11 Nov 2025); horticulture
    applicants must not have been in the UK as a Seasonal Worker in the 4 months before
    the work start; sponsored workers must be guaranteed ≥32 paid hours/week at ≥£12.71/hr.
    Sources: gov.uk Sponsor-a-Seasonal-Worker guidance v04/26; nfuonline.com scheme page;
    Commons Library CBP-9665.
  - **Post-Brexit context [V — Commons Library CBP-9665]:** free movement ended 2021; the
    EU seasonal workforce UK horticulture ran on is gone; quotas taper deliberately to
    push automation/domestic recruitment. Consequence: the *domestic* and *settled-status*
    workforce is the pool TopFarms actually serves — the scheme-operator layer handles
    the visa'd remainder. That scarcity is our demand-side tailwind, stated honestly.
  - **NMW bands (from 1 April 2026) [V — gov.uk]:** £12.71 (21+, National Living Wage) ·
    £10.85 (18–20) · £8.00 (under-18 and apprentices) · accommodation offset £11.10/day.
    The accommodation offset matters: tied housing is normal in UK farm work, and the
    offset is the legal cap on what can count against wages. Same framing rule as AU:
    surface and link, never calculate or warrant compliance.
- **Entity/tax:** Companies House incorporation (or UK establishment registration) + VAT
  (20%) on fees; replaces ABN/GST-AU. Trust chain: verification via **Companies House**
  lookup (EXPANSION-MODEL §4). [A — structure is a gate-6-equivalent legal/accounting call.]
- **Region taxonomy:** county-based, not state-based. Working seed [A]: Yorkshire ·
  Cumbria · Devon & Cornwall · Somerset/Dorset · Kent · East Anglia · Herefordshire/
  Shropshire · Lancashire/Cheshire · Wales (Carmarthenshire/Pembrokeshire) · Scotland
  (Dumfries & Galloway, Aberdeenshire) · Northern Ireland (assess separately — different
  jurisdiction for employment law [A]).
- **Credibility test:** "would a **Yorkshire farmer** read this and nod?" Voice deltas:
  UK spelling already native (config rule ports untouched); kill NZ/AU-isms (ute →
  pick-up/Land Rover, paddock → field in most counties, dairy shed → milking parlour);
  livestock idiom shifts (lambing/tupping calendar language, hill vs lowland distinction).
  Hire-2 owns the word-list, same as AU.

## 4. Competitor / channel landscape delta

Full threat model in `COMPETITIVE-DEFENCE.md` §4. The UK-specific channel facts:

- **Generalist boards:** Indeed UK and Totaljobs dominate volume [V — both live, mass-market].
- **Specialist incumbent:** **Farmers Weekly Jobs** (jobs.fwi.co.uk — 64 live vacancies
  sighted July 2026 [V]) is the established ag jobs board, owned by the Farms.com group,
  which **also owns De Lacy Executive** (UK ag recruitment consultancy, operating since
  2000) [V — delacyexecutive.co.uk; jobs.fwi.co.uk employer page]. Board + recruiter under
  one owner = the most credible fast-follow threat in the UK.
- **Specialist recruiters:** Agricultural Recruitment Specialists (agrirs.co.uk) [V],
  De Lacy Executive [V] — retained/contingency search at fees TopFarms undercuts by an
  order of magnitude (same anchor logic as the NZ $7–10k recruiter contrast, PRICING §4).
- **Scheme operators as channel, not just competitor:** the 7 operators are gatekeepers
  to seasonal labour flow — which is exactly why they're the anchor-partner design (§2),
  not an enemy list.
- **No owned-audience anywhere:** as with AU, zero owned FB-group advantage. Channel
  weight order [OPINION]: anchor accounts → NFU/AHDB-adjacent credibility → Index-UK
  SEO/content → paid last.

## 5. Hire-2 profile (delta from hire-1)

Requires hire 2. Different animal from the AU field-sales profile: a **B2B/enterprise
seller with UK agri credibility** — ex-scheme-operator account manager, estate/farm
management sector, or agri-corporate background. Owns: the anchor-account pipeline
(with founder on calls), operator/estate relationship management, UK localisation
sign-off, and the Index-UK data-partner conversations. Does NOT run volume outreach in
year one — there is no consumer motion to run until anchors are signed. 0.5–1.0 FTE,
UK salary band re-costed at entry [A — £-equivalent of the EXPANSION-MODEL §5 band].

## 6. Kill criteria delta

Same two-of-N structure and month-6 review as AU, with the B-led test replacing the
liquidity-gate tests [MODELLED]:

1. **Anchor failure:** <2 anchor accounts signed within 6 months of hire-2 starting —
   the entry premise (B-led) is falsified; do not "pivot to consumer" as a rescue, that
   is a different, more expensive entry that hasn't been gated.
2. **Terms failure:** anchors sign but refuse %/Pass pricing and won't move off
   utility-flat expectations — this is the EXPANSION-MODEL §6 crux firing in the worst
   place; escalate to strategy review, not discounting.
3. **Usage failure:** anchors signed but <20 placements/roles flowing through by month 9.
4. **Economics:** founder+hire-2 hours per anchor account >2× the PRICING §3 Segment-B
   labour model with no automation path.

A UK kill does not touch AU/NZ operations (gate discipline again) — but it does demote
the $10M arithmetic to the ~$3–4M Segment-A-weighted case (EXPANSION-MODEL §6), which
must be said out loud at the review, not buried.
