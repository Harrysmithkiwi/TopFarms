# v2.1 Milestone Scoping — "Match + Train + Retain" (Ag-Broad)

**Status:** SCOPING / DISCOVERY — not started. Gated on the sales reality check below.
**Created:** 2026-05-29
**Predecessor:** v2.0 "Launch Readiness" — functionally complete; milestone close gated only on PEND-01 (Stripe live-mode).

> This is a scoping artifact, not an executable roadmap yet. It captures the agreed v2.1 shape, scope boundaries, phase structure, and the deliberate sequencing decisions — so that when the sales precondition is met, planning can start from a settled scope rather than re-litigating it.

---

## Brand positioning (v2.1)

**"TopFarms — NZ's agricultural job marketplace, expanding to primary industries over time."**

Clear, honest, defensible. Today the platform does **matching** well. v2.1 builds the **train** pillar and begins the **retain** pillar, staying within agriculture. Other primary industries are a deliberate future-vertical roadmap, not v2.1 scope.

---

## Strategic thesis (and the key reframe)

The brand is "match, train, retain." Matching exists; train + retain are unbuilt. v2.1 tests whether **linking matching → training increases seeker engagement and employer-perceived value** — answerable cheaply.

**Key reframe (decided during discovery):** Training is delivered as a **content + features layer on the existing two-sided product**, NOT as a third marketplace side.

- ❌ **Rejected:** "Training Providers as an acquired third side" (self-service portal, provider onboarding funnel, bookings/commission). NZ ag training is a small, finite, institutional, largely-subsidised set (Primary ITO / Te Pūkenga, Lincoln, Massey, polytechs, few private). It's a **directory to curate**, not a marketplace to acquire. A commission-on-bookings model isn't real while training is government-funded and non-transactable through TopFarms.
- ✅ **Accepted:** Admin-curated training **directory** + a **skills-gap bridge** that connects the matching engine to training, reusing the skills taxonomy that already exists.

Full prior analysis (for/against, marketplace dynamics, NZ funding context, kill signals) is in the conversation record dated 2026-05-29; the conclusions are folded into this doc.

## Scope boundaries

### In scope for v2.1 (agriculture-broad)
- **Livestock:** dairy, beef, sheep, goats, pigs, poultry
- **Cropping:** arable, grain, vegetable production
- **Farm management** & agribusiness
- **Agronomy** (nutrient/soil/pasture)
- **Ag mechanics** & machinery

### Out of scope for v2.1 — future verticals (deliberate sequencing, NOT deferral)
- Horticulture, viticulture, apiculture, aquaculture, forestry, other primary industries.

**Future-vertical expansion is itself a roadmap path.** Once agriculture has real liquidity, add verticals one at a time — each brings its own skills, training categories, and directory content:
- v3.0 — Horticulture vertical
- v3.x — Viticulture
- later — apiculture / aquaculture / forestry

This is a sequencing decision: prove the model in ag-broad first, then replicate per vertical. Not "someday maybe" — a planned expansion lane.

## Phase 23 — Skills taxonomy: consolidate + expand to ~24 broad competencies (with migration mapping)

**Goal:** Replace the current ~40 dairy-heavy skills with ~22-24 **broad competency-level** skills covering agriculture broadly. Broad competencies give clean matching signal; granular detail lives in job descriptions, not the taxonomy.

**Framing principle:** each skill is a competency a seeker can self-assess and an employer can plausibly tag a job with, mapping cleanly to a training category. (With 70+ skills, gap analysis is noisy — "you're missing 23 things"; with 5-10 it's too coarse. ~24 is the sweet spot.)

### Target taxonomy — 24 competencies across 6 categories

**Livestock — animal handling & husbandry (5)**
- Dairy cattle management — milking, herd routines, calf rearing
- Beef cattle management — handling, mustering, weaning, finishing
- Sheep & lamb handling — drafting, drenching, lambing, shearing-adjacent
- Animal health & husbandry — general livestock health, drenching, treatments, basic vet care across species
- Mustering & stockmanship — moving stock, working dogs, yards

**Cropping & agronomy (4)**
- Arable & grain production — wheat, barley, maize
- Vegetable & root crop production — field veg/potato planting→harvest
- Pasture & forage management — renewal, silage, hay, grazing planning
- Agronomy & soil management — nutrient management, soil testing, fertiliser, IPM

**Machinery & equipment (4)**
- Tractor operation — driving, implements, basic maintenance
- Heavy machinery & harvest equipment — combines, balers, foragers, telehandlers
- Spraying & application equipment — boom/knapsack/mounted
- Farm vehicle handling — utes, quads, side-by-sides, off-road

**Farm operations & infrastructure (4)**
- Fencing & yard construction
- Irrigation & water systems — pivots, K-line, troughs, basic plumbing
- General farm maintenance — repairs, welding-adjacent, basic mechanical
- Fuel & chemical handling — safe storage, application, compliance

**Management & business (4)**
- Farm planning & operations management
- Staff supervision & leadership
- Farm financial management
- Compliance & record-keeping — H&S, environmental, regulatory docs

**Cross-cutting (3, +1 optional)**
- Health & safety competency
- Sustainable & regenerative practices
- Data & farm tech literacy — MINDA, FarmIQ, data entry, basic precision ag
- *(optional #25)* Customer & supplier relations — stock agents, vets, contractors, processors

Trim-to-22 option: merge "Sustainable & regenerative practices" into "Agronomy & soil management"; defer "Customer & supplier relations."

### Migration mapping (from current 40 → target ~24)

**Reality check — the migration is near-trivial today.** Verified prod state 2026-05-29:
- `seeker_skills`: **12 rows** (3 test seekers) · `job_skills`: **0 rows** · `match_scores`: 3 rows · active jobs: 1
- There is **no production skill signal to preserve** — the data is test-only and re-enterable. The mapping matters for correctness + documentation, not for protecting user data. Do it cleanly; don't over-engineer a zero-downtime data-preserving migration for 12 test rows.

**Schema gotcha (real work, not just data):** `public.skills.sector` has a CHECK constraint `sector IN ('dairy', 'sheep_beef', 'both')` (`supabase/migrations/001_initial_schema.sql:94`). Ag-broad skills (cropping, agronomy, ag mechanics) don't fit those three values. Phase 23 must **drop or redesign the `sector` column** — likely make `category` the primary axis (the 6 categories above) and either drop `sector` or repurpose it as a coarse `discipline` tag. Decide during planning.

**Mapping classes** (old skill → new competency):
- **Consolidate (many→one):** the 7 milking skills + dairy livestock skills (Herd health monitoring, Calving assistance, AI, Body condition scoring, Calf rearing, Feeding systems management) → **Dairy cattle management**. The mustering trio → **Mustering & stockmanship**. The two fencing skills → **Fencing & yard construction**. Two tractor skills → **Tractor operation**. ATV/quad + farm motorbike → **Farm vehicle handling**.
- **Net-new (no current equivalent):** all of Cropping & agronomy (current taxonomy has ZERO crop skills), Heavy machinery & harvest equipment, Spraying & application equipment, Fuel & chemical handling, Data & farm tech literacy, Beef cattle management (partial overlap with stock handling).
- **Relocate, don't map:** the 5 `DairyNZ Level 1-5` (`category='qualification'`) are **credentials, not competencies** → move to credential/qualification tracking in **Phase 26**, do not carry into the competency taxonomy.
- **Drop/fold:** Effluent system management → folds into General farm maintenance or Irrigation & water systems.

**Migration steps (planning-level):**
1. Alter `skills` schema (drop/redesign `sector` CHECK; confirm `category` enum/values).
2. Insert the ~24 new competencies.
3. Map the 12 test `seeker_skills` rows to new skill_ids (or simply clear + let test seekers re-tag — acceptable given test-only data).
4. `job_skills` is empty — nothing to map.
5. Recompute `match_scores` (3 rows; derived).
6. Document old→new mapping table in the phase SUMMARY for traceability.

**Analytics scaffolding (this phase):** stand up the counters that Phases 24/25 will report against (directory views, gap-prompt impressions/clicks, lead events) using the existing `platform_stats`-style RPC pattern. Zero new infra.

## Phase 24 — Skills-gap analysis (the match → train bridge)

On a job-detail / match view, compute `job_skills − seeker_skills` (pure SQL set difference — **no LLM, no new API cost**) and surface the gap with training links.

**Worked example** (operator's, at the right granularity): a dairy job tags 7 competencies; a seeker self-tags 4 → "You're missing: Pasture & forage management, General farm maintenance, Health & safety competency. Recommended training: [directory links tagged with these]." Clean, actionable signal — only possible because the taxonomy is broad (~24), not granular.

Reuses: `job_skills`, `seeker_skills`, `match_scores`, existing matching logic. Net-new: the gap UI + the join from gap → directory entries (Phase 25).

---

## Phase 25 — Training directory + funding navigation

Admin-curated directory of NZ ag training, filterable by **category + region + format**, tagged with the **same skills taxonomy** so Phase 24 gap-analysis links straight in.

**Directory categories (ag-only for v2.1):** Livestock & animal production · Crop production & agronomy · Farm management & agribusiness · Ag mechanics & technology · Sustainable & emerging practices · Specialized ag training. *(Exclude horticulture/viticulture/apiculture — future verticals.)*

**Funding navigation:** because much NZ ag training is subsidised (fees-free, apprenticeship/levy-funded), the directory should surface funding context per entry (e.g. "Primary ITO — employer-levy funded", "Te Pūkenga — fees-free eligible") rather than prices. This is content, not a payments integration.

**Lead capture ("express interest"):** seeker clicks interested → logs a lead → admin queue + manual email follow-up (existing Resend, low volume, free-tier-safe). **NOT bookings.** Mirrors the Phase 21 doc-verification queue pattern.

Providers are **curated content** (admin-entered), not an acquired marketplace side. Initial content = the known finite NZ ag provider set; one-afternoon curation, no provider acquisition funnel.

---

## Phase 26 — Credential / expiry tracking (retain pillar)

Track seeker credentials (GROWSAFE pesticide, machinery tickets, first-aid, the relocated DairyNZ levels) with expiry alerts.

Reuses: `seeker_documents` (exists), the relocated qualification data from Phase 23, **Resend + pg_cron** for expiry alerts (both already in use; low volume, free-tier-safe). **Scope tight: track + alert, do NOT verify** credentials. Strong real-world NZ fit; clearest "retain" feature.

---

## Zero-added-cost constraint (hard, restated)

Every phase above uses **only what's already paid for**: existing Supabase tables/RLS/RPC patterns, existing Edge Functions, existing Resend (low-volume lead + expiry emails, free-tier-safe), existing pg_cron, **no LLM calls** (gap analysis is SQL), Vercel/Supabase/Cloudflare all on current tiers. No new SaaS, no new paid APIs, no premium upgrades.

If any feature later genuinely needs a paid integration to deliver real value (e.g. real bookings/payments), it is surfaced honestly and deferred to a **monetised phase** — no half-built fakes.

## Deferred / rejected (with reasons)

| Item | Disposition | Reason |
|------|-------------|--------|
| Three-sided marketplace (provider self-service, provider acquisition funnel) | **Reject** for v2.1 | Providers are curated content, not an acquired side; NZ ag training is finite + institutional |

## Sales-first gating (precondition for starting v2.1 engineering)

**Get real ag employers posting real jobs before sinking deep into v2.1 engineering.** Current prod: 1 active job (a UAT test), 0 paid listings, ~1 real employer, 0 `job_skills` rows. The two-sided core has no liquidity.

The cheap v2.1 slices (taxonomy + skills-gap) are defensible even at low liquidity because they strengthen the seeker value prop for little eng cost. But a *large* training build while the matching core is empty is the wrong priority. The honest question to keep asking: *is training the right thing to build next, or the more fun thing to build next?*

**Recommended gate:** do not start Phase 23 execution until there are **≥ N real employer-posted ag jobs** in prod (pick N during planning — even 5-10 real jobs changes the picture). Until then, v2.1 stays in scoping and sales/acquisition is the priority.

---

## Kill / pivot signals (instrument from Phase 23's analytics scaffolding)

- Directory views ≈ 0 after launch → seekers don't want training discovery here; stop, don't build deeper.
- Gap prompts shown but ignored (low click-through) → match→train linkage isn't valued; pivot.
- Training work pulling eng cycles while matching core stalls → revert focus to liquidity.
- Directory content rotting (Te Pūkenga is actively reorganising) → if no admin freshness discipline holds, the directory lies within a year; enforce `last_reviewed_at` or cut it.
