# Founder brand — publishing plan

*2026-07-08 · Stage-2 M3. The audit's D-grade dimension: zero owned distribution despite
"founder trust is the channel" being the whole GTM thesis. This plan fixes the mechanics.
Voice rules inherit from `TopFarms_Outreach_Reply_Config.md` — saleyard test applies to
every word published.*

## Positioning

**The person who actually knows what's happening in NZ farm hiring.** Workforce thinker,
not vendor. Harry publishes what the data shows and what farmers tell him — the platform
is the byline, not the pitch.

## The four pillars (rotate; never invent)

1. **The numbers** — Index cuts: demand by role and region, wage bands, accommodation
   rates, fill times. Small-n honesty is the brand: "17 posts this month said X" beats a
   fake percentage. Source: INDEX-SPEC monthly cut.
2. **The stories** — anonymised placement and mismatch tales. Per-name written consent
   before any name appears (config rule). Until real placements exist: the *seeker-post
   patterns* from the demand study are publishable ("what 35 job-hunting posts told us").
3. **The stance** — workers never pay; the Match Gap (migrant seekers vs visa-gated
   farms); why hiring shouldn't live in comment threads.
4. **The build** — solo founder + AI operating notes. Differentiates with the
   tech-adjacent audience, future hires, and partner orgs; farmers skip it, that's fine.

## The flywheel

**Tuesday email (weekly, non-negotiable)** → monthly **Index post** (public page + PR
push) → quarterly **State of NZ Farm Work** (the licensing teaser + media moment).
Every group post, DM signature, invoice footer, and site footer feeds the list.
List capture: landing-page email field + group pinned post + Index page gate-free
(gating kills PR value; subscribe prompt only).

- **Tuesday email shape (20-min production):** one number (Index/pipeline), one story or
  stance paragraph, one ask (reply/refer/list a job). Plain text. Signed Harry.
  First edition: FA-08 — send it with whatever this week's true numbers are, however
  small. "3 farms, 14 seekers, 1 placement in flight" is credible; silence isn't.
- **LinkedIn 2×/wk:** recycled from the email (pillar 1 or 4). Zero new writing.
- **Group posts 2×/wk:** pillar 2/3 flavoured for seekers (existing rhythm doc cadence).
- **Quarterly talk/PR:** only once the Index exists — pitch rural media (The Country,
  Farmers Weekly, REX) with the quarterly cut. No Index, no pitch.

## Cross-market note (forward)

The *person* brand doesn't port; the *format* does. At AU entry, hire-1 fronts an AU
edition of the email/Index under the TopFarms masthead ("requires hire 1" — per
TEAM-AUTOMATION-PLAN). Do not fake local voice from Hamilton.

## Measures (monthly, in the metrics dashboard)

List size · open-to-reply rate (replies, not opens — MPP-safe per metrics-dashboard.md) ·
Index page visits → email signups · inbound conversations attributed. Kill rule: if the
email misses 3 consecutive Tuesdays, the plan has failed operationally — diagnose founder
load, don't quietly drop cadence.
