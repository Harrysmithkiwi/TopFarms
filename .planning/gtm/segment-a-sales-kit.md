# Segment A sales kit: discovery + close for owner-operators

*2026-07-08 · Segment A ONLY (asset discipline, LOCKED #5). No enterprise variants, no Segment-B pricing talk, ever. Voice per `docs/_canonical/TopFarms_Outreach_Reply_Config.md`: the saleyard test on every line, no em dashes, NZ spelling, understate. Offer terms in `founding-25-offer.md`. It is calving (LOCKED #10): expect "too busy", and treat it as a routing signal, not a rejection.*

## The phone-first rule

Any warm or qualified employer reply moves to the phone. The phone is the ag trust channel; a farmer decides whether you are real in the first thirty seconds of hearing you. Never a calendar link, never "grab a slot". The line is: **"No rush. If you are keen, tell me a day that suits and I will give you a ring."** Never call 4–7am or 3–6pm (milking). Best windows: 10am–2pm, evenings after 7pm if they offered.

## The 10-minute discovery call

Ten minutes is the promise and the discipline. A farmer who gives you ten and gets ten trusts you with thirty next time. This is a flow, not a script; say it your way each time.

### 1. Open (30s)

Who you are, why you are calling, how long it takes. Confident, not apologetic.

> "Harry here, I run TopFarms. You put your hand up about the [role] listing / we swapped messages about your [role]. Have you got ten minutes now, or should I ring back after milking?"

If it is a bad time, book the day, not the minute. "Tomorrow late morning" beats "10:15am".

### 2. Their last hire story (3 min, the heart of the call)

Get them talking about the last time they hired. Listen more than you talk. Questions, not pitch:

- "How did you find your last person?"
- "How long did the seat sit empty?"
- "How many of the messages you got were actually worth a call back?"
- "Who covered the roster while you were looking?"

**Log their answers verbatim after the call** (lab question 2, the anchor quotes live or die here).

### 3. What it cost them (2 min, their numbers, not ours)

Draw out the cost of the vacancy in their own terms. Time, missed milkings, the relief they had to beg for, the partner doing 5am shifts. Do NOT lead with our price and do NOT quote recruiter fees at them as a scare; ask and let them anchor themselves:

- "What did that stretch cost you, do you reckon? Not just money, the whole thing."
- "Ever used a recruiter or an agency for farm staff? How did that go?"

If they volunteer a recruiter number, that is a gift. Write it down word for word.

### 4. How TopFarms works (2 min, plain)

Three things, in this order, no more:

1. **Match:** "You put the job up with what actually matters, the must-haves. It puts the job in front of people who fit them, so you are not sorting a hundred messages for three real ones."
2. **Verify:** "People on there have real profiles, real experience listed. You see who they are before you pick up the phone."
3. **Workers never pay:** "It is free for workers, always. That is why the good ones are on it."

No AI pitch. No "algorithm". If they ask how the matching works: "It reads what you asked for and what they can do, and lines them up. The short version is you get fewer, better applicants."

### 5. The Founding-25 ask (2 min)

Now money is fine (they are warm, this is not a cold contact). Full offer, stated plainly, per `founding-25-offer.md`:

> "Here is what I am doing with the first 25 farms. First listing is free, so it costs you nothing to see it work. If you hire someone through it, the fee is $350 [lab: test $300/$400], and only when you hire, invoiced after. And if we cannot put three people in front of you who meet what you asked for, you do not pay it. Founding farms get my mobile, a WhatsApp group with the other founding farms, and a dinner each quarter on me. In return, once it has worked, I would like to tell your story, and nothing goes out with your name on it without your written ok."

Then stop talking. Silence is the close. Log the response verbatim (accepted / price objection / declined).

### 6. Next step (30s)

One concrete action, theirs to pick:

- **Yes:** "Good. What is the role? I will help you get the listing up today, takes ten minutes." (Do it on the phone if they are willing. Activation now beats activation later.)
- **Interested, not now:** "No rush. When does calving ease up for you? I will ring you in [month]." Book it in Asana before you hang up.
- **Calving-slammed but short-staffed NOW:** pivot to the rush tier (relief milkers / calf rearers, see `rush-tier-2026.md`), and book the full walk-through for October.

## Objection handling (config voice, never combative)

The pattern every time: agree with the true part, add the one thing they have not counted, offer the low-risk next step. Never bash how they hire now.

**"Facebook's free."**
> "It is, and it works for plenty of farms. The bit it does not do is the sorting. You post, you get eighty messages, and somewhere in there are the three worth calling. TopFarms does the sorting for you. And your first listing is free, so you can run both and see which one hands you better people."

**"Never heard of you."**
> "Fair enough, we are new. A few Waikato dairy farms are on it now. I am not asking you to take it on faith, the first listing is free and if it does not put good people in front of you, it has cost you nothing and I will not bother you again."
*(Named farms only with logged written consent; until then the unnamed line above, per the consent log.)*

**"What if the person's useless?"**
> "Two answers. The profiles show real experience, so you see what they have actually done before you ring them. And the guarantee: if we cannot put three people in front of you who meet the must-haves you set, you do not pay the placement fee. You are not paying for a punt, you pay when you hire someone."

**"I only hire once a year."**
> "Then that one hire matters more, not less. One bad season-opener costs you the season. There is nothing to keep paying, no subscription, you use it when you hire and it costs nothing to have the listing sitting there the once a year you need it."

**"Is this one of those AI things?"**
> "There is some clever matching under the hood, same as anything modern. But what it is, is a hiring site built for NZ farms. You write what you need, it puts the job in front of people who fit, you pick who to ring. No robot hires anyone."
*(Honest, no AI as a selling point, no denial either.)*

**"Too busy, it's calving."**
> "Yeah, I figured, and I will keep it short. Two things. If you are short-handed right now, relief milkers and calf rearers are exactly what we are placing this month, that is fast and small and I can help you today. The full look at how it works can wait, when does it ease off for you, October? I will ring you then."
*(Rush-tier pivot + book October. Never argue with calving.)*

**"What's it cost?" (asked early, before beat 5)**
> Answer it, never dodge: "First listing is free. If you hire someone through it, $350 [lab: test $300/$400], only when you hire. Can I give you the ten-second version of how it gets you the right people first?" Then return to the flow.

## What NOT to say (one paragraph, memorise it)

Never: disrupt, revolutionise, seamless, frictionless, leverage, ecosystem, world-class, synergy, empower, unlock, harness, talent pool, end-to-end, "the leading platform". Never money, price or "free" in a cold first message (the signup page and the warm call carry pricing). Never pitch AI. Never bash Facebook, word of mouth or the local grapevine. Never echo a private hiring reason (illness, death, breakdown, money trouble), respond to the job, blind to the difficulty. Never name a farm to another farm without written consent on the log. Never promise "the perfect hire" or "guaranteed quality staff", the guarantee is three qualified applicants, quote it exactly. And never, under any circumstances, talk Segment-B pricing (percentages, season passes, master agreements) to anyone: if a multi-farm or 5-plus-hires operation surfaces, log it as an enterprise signal for the founder and say only "let me come back to you properly on that". Every line you do say has to pass the test: would I say this to someone I just met at a saleyard?
