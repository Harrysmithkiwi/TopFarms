## The offer in one breath

You get in as one of the first 25 farms on TopFarms. Your first listing is free. If you hire someone through us, the placement fee is $350 [lab: test $300/$400]. If we cannot put 3 qualified applicants in front of you, you do not pay it. In return you let us tell your story, with your written say-so on every use of your name.

1. **Founding Employer badge** on their farm profile. Permanent. First 25 only, then it closes.
2. **Harry's mobile.** Not a support inbox. The founder, on the phone, when hiring is going sideways.
3. **Private Founding-25 WhatsApp group.** 25 farms comparing notes on hiring, pay rates, what is working. Harry in the room.
4. **Quarterly dinner.** Founding farms, Harry's shout, somewhere central. War stories and roadmap input.
5. **The fill guarantee** (below). Every listing they run while a Founding Employer.
6. **First listing free.** Same as every employer. No special discount beyond it, and we say so plainly (see FAQ).

## The guarantee, precisely worded

**"3 qualified applicants or you don't pay."**

- **Qualified means:** the applicant meets the stated must-haves of the listing. The must-haves are the requirements written into the listing when it goes live (for example: 2+ seasons dairy experience, full NZ driver licence, available from date X). Not vibes, not our judgement after the fact, the listing's own words.
- **You don't pay covers:** the **placement fee**. If a listing draws fewer than 3 applicants who meet its stated must-haves, and the farm still hires someone through TopFarms off that listing, the $350 placement fee is waived on that hire.
- **It does not cover:** the listing fee. A paid listing ($100/$150/$200) is payment for the listing running, and it is not refunded. The first listing is free anyway, so for most Founding farms the guarantee means their first hire risks exactly nothing.
- **Scope:** per listing, while the listing is live. Placement fees are already success-billed (no hire, no fee), so the guarantee is on top of that, not instead of it.

Plain version for the phone: "If we can't put three people in front of you who actually meet what you asked for, the placement fee is off. You only ever pay it when you hire someone anyway."

## What we ask back

- **Written per-name consent, per use** (config consent rule + the consent log in `outreach-templates.md`). Scope logged each time: "ok to name us to other farms" / "ok in a case study" / "logo only". No name is ever used without a row in the log. They can say no to any use and stay a Founding Employer.

## FAQ (config voice, say it like this)

**"Why isn't it free if we're your first customers?"**
Because free tells you nothing and costs you nothing, and then it is worth nothing to either of us. Your first listing is free so you can see it work before a dollar moves. After that, the fee only lands when you actually hire someone, and if we cannot put three people in front of you who meet what you asked for, you do not pay it. I would rather earn 25 farms that pay because it worked than 25 that signed up because it was free.

**"What do I actually get for the $350?"** [lab: test $300/$400]
A hire. That is the only time you pay it. Compare it with what a recruiter runs, or what a month with a seat empty at calving costs you, and it is not close.

**"What if nobody good applies?"**
Then you do not pay the placement fee. That is the guarantee, and it is in writing.

**"What's the catch on the case study?"**
No catch, and no surprises. Nothing with your name on it goes anywhere without your written yes for that specific use, every time. Plenty of farms just do the anonymous version.

**"When does Founding 25 close?"**
At 25 farms. Then the badge, the mobile, the WhatsApp group and the dinners are closed for good. Pricing after that is the same, the perks are not.

## INTERNAL: the pricing lab protocol (never shown to a farmer)

This cohort is the WTP lab from `docs/commercial/PRICING-MODEL.md` §9 (FA-07: book the first 10 conversations). We never call a farmer a lab subject. We run the offer honestly and log what happens.

### The 5 lab questions (PRICING-MODEL §9, in order)

1. Does $350–400 flat clear **without price objection** at ≥60%?
2. What do owner-operators **anchor against, verbatim**? (recruiter cost / vacancy pain / Facebook-free / something else)
3. Does the **guarantee shift the yes-rate** materially?
4. How many **listings does a paying employer post in 90 days**?
5. Do any **enterprise-profile accounts surface**? (≥5 hires/yr or ≥3 farms asking for terms → Segment-B trigger §6a. Do not pitch them Segment-B pricing; log it and flag to the founder.)

### How to log

- **Verbatim or it didn't happen.** Straight after each call (not during), write the exact words they used about price, in quotes, into the Asana card comment AND a running lab log (one file/sheet, one row per conversation: date · farm · price presented ($300/$350/$400) · accepted / price objection / declined · verbatim anchor quote · guarantee raised by them Y/N · listings intent · enterprise signal Y/N).
- Paraphrase goes in notes; the anchor quote itself stays verbatim. Question 2 is answered by quotes, not summaries.
- Alternate the price presented across conversations so $300 and $400 both get real airtime [lab marker]. Never present two prices to the same farm.

### The decision rule (pre-written, no post-hoc fiddling)

After the first 10 conversations (FA-07), and re-checked at 25:

| Result | Action |
|---|---|
| **≥60% accept** ($350–400 without price objection) | **Lock $400.** Grandfather the cohort at what each accepted. |
| **30–60% accept** | **Test $300 and/or a stronger guarantee variant** on the next tranche before locking. |
| **<30% accept** | **Fix framing before retreating on price** (per PRICING-MODEL §5): re-anchor on recruiter cost and vacancy pain, lead with the guarantee, re-run. Price retreat is the last lever, not the first. |

If the fill guarantee triggers on >15% of placements, stop touching price entirely and fix supply density/verification (PRICING-MODEL §5, last row).
