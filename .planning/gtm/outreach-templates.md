# TopFarms GTM — Outreach Templates

*2026-07-02 · Every template obeys `TopFarms_Outreach_Reply_Config.md`: NZ spelling, no em dashes, first-person Harry, understate, read their actual post, no AI pitch, never bash how they hire now, never echo a private hiring reason. These are guides to write from, not scripts to blast. Harry approves + sends every one.*

## Global rules for these templates

- **Vary every send** (config variation kit). Identical copy at volume gets spotted in a small community.
- **Employer cold first contact = MONEY-FREE.** No price, no "free", no fees. Money lives on the signup page + a later post-signup message.
- **Seeker messages CAN say "free / workers never pay"** — for a worker that is reassurance, not a price ask. It is the point.
- **Named testimonials require documented per-name consent.** Until you have it, use the unnamed line: *"a few Waikato dairy farms are on it now"*. Never name a farm to another farm without their yes on record.
- **No link in the cold first FB DM** (Message Requests spam-flags links and they will not preview). Plain text. Links are fine in email/Seek and in warm FB replies.

## A. EMPLOYER — Facebook DM (Lane B: their post has no contact details)

Five beats (config §4): specific hook → who Harry is → one matched benefit → clear active CTA (in warm/later touches) → human sign-off. Cold first DM is plain text, no link.

**Cold, day 0 (plain text, no link, no money):**
> Hey, saw you're after a farm assistant up your way near Taupō. I run TopFarms, it matches farm staff to jobs like yours so you are not left sorting through a pile of messages to find the right one. Happy to tell you how it works if it would be useful. Good luck either way, Harry

**Follow-up 1, +3 days (different angle, still no link):**
> Morning, just following up on the herd manager role. The rotary experience is the hard part to find, I know. TopFarms matches people on the actual experience you are after rather than whoever happens to be about. No worries if it is not for you.

**Follow-up 2, +7 days (now a warm-ish touch, link ok if they engaged):**
> Last quick one from me. If you want to put the role in front of a few good people directly, you can stick it up here: [link]. Either way, hope you get sorted.

**Breakup, +14 days:**
> All good, I will leave you to it. If hiring gets painful down the track, TopFarms is here. Cheers, Harry

## B. EMPLOYER — Facebook DM (Lane A: contact given / warm reply)

Same voice, you can be a touch more direct and include the link earlier since they engaged. Lead with the specific role, one benefit matched to their post, the plain active CTA ("you can put your job up here: [link]"), warm sign-off. Still no money.

## C. EMPLOYER — Email / Seek / Trade Me (longer, more formal, 4-touch)

More formal than FB. Seek/Trade Me: publicly-stated contact only, keep it light, ToS-careful. **CTA uses the phone, farmer register (adapted McKenna principle): no calendar-grab, no single specific slot.**

**The CTA line to reuse (gold, adapted):**
> No rush. If you are keen, tell me a day that suits and I will give you a ring.

**Touch 1 — Day 1 (personal, under 120 words, subject honest + specific):**
> Subject: your herd manager role, Ashburton
>
> Hi [name], saw your ad for a herd manager down in Ashburton. Finding someone with the right rotary experience is usually the hard part. I run TopFarms, an NZ-built site that matches farm staff to jobs on the actual experience you need, not just whoever is looking. A few Waikato dairy farms are on it now. No rush, if you are keen, tell me a day that suits and I will give you a ring. Cheers, Harry

**Touch 2 — Day 4 (bump the day-1, one line):**
> Hi [name], just checking this reached you. Happy to keep it to a quick phone call whenever suits.

**Touch 3 — Day 9 (pure value, no ask):**
> Hi [name], no pitch here, just thought this might be useful. Median [role] pay around [region] is sitting near [$X] going into calving. Happy to send the regional breakdown if handy.

**Touch 4 — Day 14 (breakup, highest reply rate):**
> Hi [name], I will stop bothering you after this one. If hiring gets painful this season, TopFarms is here and I am a phone call away. All the best, Harry

**Video-CTA (email / Seek / warm FB reply only, NOT cold FB DM):**
> If it is easier than a call, I can send a 90-second video showing exactly how it works. Want me to flick it over?

## D. SEEKER — Facebook DM (net-new voice, approved principles)

**Seeker voice (ratified):** warmer, shorter, FB-casual. You are doing them a favour, not selling. **Free / no recruiter fees is allowed and encouraged.** Frustration is framed as **shared/observed, never pointed at what this seeker is doing wrong** (no "stop messaging into the void" aimed at them). Same carry-over rules: NZ spelling, no em dashes, first-person, no bashing how they job-hunt, no AI pitch, "no worries if not".

**Cold, day 0 (supplement DM, ~30/day cap, plain text):**
> Saw you are on the hunt for dairy work around Waikato. Reckon you will get sorted. Lot of good workers getting overlooked on FB right now, so I built TopFarms, you put up a free profile and farms come to you off what you can actually do. Free for workers, always. Have a look if you want. Good luck either way, Harry

**Follow-up, +4 days:**
> No pressure at all, just in case it is handy. Takes a couple of minutes to put a profile up and it is free for workers. Either way, hope the search goes well.

**Optional breakup:**
> All good, I will leave you to it. If the FB search gets tiring, TopFarms is there whenever. Cheers, Harry

**Visa-holder angle (swap the benefit line):**
> ...no recruiter fees, ever. Workers never pay a cent on TopFarms.

**Relief/seasonal angle (swap the benefit line):**
> ...good way to line up next season before you finish this one.

## E. Post-click / post-signup assets (where money lives)

- **Signup page (employer):** this is where "first listing free, $100 after that" belongs (Playbook specificity: "first listing free, $100 after that" beats "competitive pricing"). Plain, concrete.
- **Post-signup follow-up (employer):** once they have signed up, a warm message can mention the Founding Employer offer (50% off Featured for life) and the founder's mobile. Money is welcome here, never in the cold DM.
- **Parked (revenue decision, not comms):** whether to waive the first placement fee as an acquisition wedge. If used, it belongs in a later post-signup message, not the cold one.

## Consent log (for named testimonials)

Keep a simple record: farm name · contact · date consent given · scope ("ok to name us to other farms" / "ok in a case study" / "logo only"). No name goes into outreach without a row here. Until then: unnamed honest line only.
