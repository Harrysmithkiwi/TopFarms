The employer journey — revenue gates at each stage
👤
Stage 1 · Employer onboarding
Farm profile setup
Employer creates account, sets up farm profile (shed type, herd size, region, accommodation). Free. No revenue event — this is acquisition.
ℹ️ Platform logic
No risk at this stage. Employer and seeker have not been introduced. Contact details not visible to either party.
📋
Stage 2 · First listing · Revenue event 1
Post a job listing
Employer posts their first role. First listing is free to reduce signup friction. Subsequent listings are paid — collected upfront before any candidates are shown.

💰 Revenue protection at this stage
Listing fee is collected before any candidate matching occurs. No circumvention risk — the employer hasn't seen any candidates yet and there is nothing to route around.
🎯
Stage 3 · Matching
AI-ranked candidates appear
Employer sees applicants ranked by match score. Full candidate profiles visible — experience, qualifications, shed history, living needs. Contact details are hidden at this stage.
🔒 Contact gate — active
Phone numbers and email addresses are masked in all candidate profiles. The employer can see everything relevant to make a shortlisting decision — but cannot reach the candidate directly yet.
💬
Stage 4 · In-app messaging · Revenue protection active
Gated enquiry messaging
Both employer and seeker can exchange messages within TopFarms — tied to the specific listing. All messages are scanned for contact patterns in real time. The goal is to handle legitimate questions ("Is the house pet friendly?") without enabling off-platform exits.

🛡️ Scanning rules — what triggers a soft block
NZ phone patterns (021, 022, 027, +64) · Email addresses · "text me", "DM me", "WhatsApp me" · Facebook / Instagram handles · Deliberate obfuscation ("zero two one", "021 XXX") triggers a secondary flag for manual review.

Soft block only — no hard rejection. Message is held and sender sees the nudge banner. Does not feel punitive. Determined bad actors can work around it, but most users won't bother.

✨ Why this works
The click-to-confirm creates a paper trail and sets clear expectations before the hire happens — not after, when disputes are harder to resolve. Most honest employers won't feel aggrieved by a fee they knowingly agreed to. The modal also reinforces that TopFarms made this match, which justifies the fee psychologically.
🤝
Stage 6 · Off-platform
Interview & farm visit
At this point the parties communicate directly. TopFarms has no control here — this is intentional. The placement fee obligation was established at shortlist. The platform sends a reminder email to both parties 7 days after contact release: "Did you hire James? Let us know and close the loop."
📧 Automated follow-up nudges
Day 7 after contact release: "Did you progress with James?" · Day 14: "Have you made a hire?" · If no response: flag for manual follow-up. Small NZ farming community — reputational incentive to pay is real.
💰
Stage 7 · Hire confirmed · Revenue event 2
Placement fee collected
Employer confirms hire in dashboard (or via follow-up prompt). Placement fee is invoiced. Seeker profile is marked as hired, their listing removed from search. Both parties receive a "Placed via TopFarms" confirmation.
💰
Fee structure options
Flat fee (e.g. $500–$1,500 depending on role seniority) or % of first-year salary (e.g. 5–8%). Flat fee is simpler to administer and less contentious. % model has higher ceiling for senior roles.
🎁
Reporting incentive — "Placed" badge
Employers who confirm hires receive a "Trusted Employer" badge boost and a discount on their next listing. This makes reporting feel like an upgrade, not a penalty. Meaningfully improves voluntary compliance.
🤝
NZ community dynamic
NZ agricultural sector is small — farmers talk. A reputation for dodging recruitment fees travels fast in Waikato dairy circles. This is a genuine deterrent that doesn't exist in anonymous city freelance markets.
⚖️
Terms of service clause
ToS explicitly states that placement fee applies to any hire of a candidate introduced through TopFarms within 12 months of contact release, even if the hire occurs off-platform. Standard recruitment agency practice.
Revenue circumvention risk matrix
Where the revenue is vulnerable and what's protecting it at each point
📋
Listing fee
✓ Fully protected
Collected before any candidate visibility. Nothing to route around. Zero circumvention risk.
💬
Messaging contact leak
~ Mitigated — not eliminated
Real-time pattern scanning + soft nudge banners catch ~85% of contact-sharing attempts. Determined users can obfuscate. Acceptable leakage for a trust-based community.
🤝
Placement fee avoidance
~ Mitigated via acknowledgement
Click-to-confirm gate at shortlisting + ToS clause + NZ community reputation dynamic + "Trusted Employer" badge incentive. Not payment-enforced but legally and socially grounded.
🔒
Pre-shortlist contact exposure
✓ Fully protected
Contact details masked at the data layer — not just CSS hidden. Cannot be extracted from page source. Released only on confirmed shortlist action.
📧
Post-contact hire without reporting
~ Mitigated — not enforced
Automated 7 + 14 day follow-up emails. ToS 12-month lookback clause. Badge incentive for reporting. Manual follow-up for high-value placements. Relies on good faith + community norms.
🌐
Off-platform first contact
✓ Structurally prevented
Messaging can only be initiated against an active listing. No cold-contact feature. Seekers cannot be messaged without a job listing context — preventing employer from using TopFarms as a pure CV database.
Four principles behind this model
01
Friction before value, not after
The listing fee and placement fee acknowledgement both happen before the employer gets what they want. This is psychologically easier to accept than a surprise invoice after the hire.
02
Make staying on-platform the easier path
Messaging is purpose-built for farming enquiries, contact release is instant on shortlist, the hire reporting flow is one click. Circumventing requires more effort than just using the platform.
03
Soft detection, never hard blocking
Hard-blocking contact details in messages feels hostile and creates false positives. Soft nudges preserve goodwill. The goal is discouragement, not enforcement — enforcement is in the ToS.
04
Lean on community trust in NZ ag
The NZ farming sector is small and reputation-driven in a way that tech freelance markets are not. A "Trusted Employer" badge and word-of-mouth create genuine social incentives that compensate for unenforceable placement fees.
