# Datum

**Philosophy:** A single value, fixed and measured, presented with the reverence usually reserved for a physical constant. Meaning made monumental by precision.

This movement treats one number as a benchmark — a surveyed datum from which everything else is measured. The composition is built like an instrument plate or a specimen sheet: a precise frame, registration marks at the corners, a fine measure-rule of patiently repeated ticks, and whisper-quiet clinical annotations in monospace. The discipline is imaginary but the rigor is real; the piece should read as though lifted from the field notebook of an exacting surveyor who decided a matter of fairness deserved the same exactitude as a matter of fact. The single glyph at the centre is not decoration — it is the origin, the zero, the point everything is referenced against. Every alignment is deliberate, every interval considered, the product of painstaking attention and master-level execution.

**Space and form.** Vast, deliberate negative space around one dominant form. A thin inset frame turns the page into a plate; the monument sits dead on the optical centre, its baseline coincident with a measured rule. Nothing floats — every element is registered to an invisible grid, and the grid is felt even where it is not drawn. The breathing room is structural, not empty: it is the silence that lets a single mark carry weight.

**Color and material.** A near-black ground in the deepest house green, honest and flat — no gradient, no gloss, no false depth. One luminous accent carries the monument and the corner marks; white and a single off-white tier carry the human sentence and its footnote. The palette is severely limited so that hierarchy comes from value and scale alone, calibrated with the patience of someone at the very top of their craft. The accent is rationed — its scarcity is its authority.

**Scale and rhythm.** Two registers only: the monumental and the microscopic. The central value is enormous, optically tuned, its counters and stroke weighting treated like a cut letter on a building. Around it, the annotations shrink to the size of footnotes and survey ticks — repeated at exact intervals, building meaning through quiet accumulation. The measure-rule's marks are identical and regular; their repetition is the patient labor that makes the centre feel earned. A short, plain human sentence anchors the piece beneath the monument, set tight as a single block so the statement and its quiet footnote read as one breath.

**Composition and balance.** The eye lands on the constant, drops to the sentence, and only then notices the apparatus of measurement framing it all — the corner ticks, the rule, the coordinates. That sequence is engineered, the inevitability the hardest thing to achieve. The finished plate must look meticulously crafted, labored over for countless hours, every interval and hairline resolved to the pixel — pristine, exact, museum-grade, an artifact that proves an ethical promise can be measured, fixed, and held as a constant.
